Farrow Ulven is a philosopher specializing in social and political philosophy with broader interests in the history of philosophy, metaphysics, and philosophy of science. Farrow completed a B.A. in philosophy at the University of Minnesota in 2020 with a thesis comparing and historicizing the respective accounts of individuation in the work of John Duns Scotus and Gottfried Leibniz. Presently, Farrow is teaching and pursuing a doctorate in philosophy at the University of Iowa.

Current projects include the development of a digital resource featuring a new translation of the first part of Spinoza's Ethics co-presented with the Latin of the Opera Posthumana which diplays color-coded textual differences between the OP and the recently uncovered manuscript held at the Vatican Library. This resource will further host an artistic rendering of the primary definitions, axioms, and propositions of the Ethics as well as an attempt at rendering each in modern logical form.
